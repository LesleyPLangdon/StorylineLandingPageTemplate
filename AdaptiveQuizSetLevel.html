Adaptive Quiz — Set‑Level Model

This adaptive assessment evaluates learner performance in sets of three questions and adjusts the difficulty level for the next set based on results. Learners begin with three moderate‑difficulty questions:

  • 3 correct → move to a set of three hard questions
  • 2 correct → continue with moderate questions
  • 0–1 correct → move to three easy questions

After each set, performance is re‑evaluated, ensuring that learners work at a difficulty level that is appropriately challenging while avoiding unnecessary frustration.

The design supports mastery learning by providing a tailored challenge path and draws on principles from Bloom’s Mastery Learning Theory and Vygotsky’s Zone of Proximal Development (ZPD). Grouping questions by difficulty level in short, consistent cycles also leverages chunking to help learners focus on performance within manageable segments.

Built in Articulate Storyline 360, this project demonstrates:

  • Conditional branching logic using variables and triggers
  • Difficulty‑based question grouping and navigation control
  • Variable tracking of total correct answers, correct answers per set, and performance by difficulty level
  • Slide templates for rapid question development
  • Clear, minimal player navigation for focused assessment delivery

This model works well as a standalone assessment or as part of a larger course, providing targeted difficulty adjustment at logical checkpoints.
